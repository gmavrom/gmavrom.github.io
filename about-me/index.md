## A few words about me

<img align="left" src="http://mavromatidis.me/downloads/Mavromatidis_circle.png"  width="120" style="margin: 15px 20px 20px 0px">

My name is Georgios Mavromatidis and I am from [Kilkis, Greece][]. 

I spent my whole childhood in this little Greek town and in 2005 I moved to Thessaloniki for my university studies. Five years later, and in 2010, I graduated with a Diploma in [Mechanical Engineering][] from [Aristotle University of Thessaloniki][]. After my graduation I had the opportunity to work as a research assistant in the group of [Prof. Dr. Agis M. Papadopoulos][].

One year after my Mechanical Engineering studies, I moved to London, UK to pursue further studies at [Imperial College London][], from where I graduated with an [MSc in Sustainable Energy Futures][] in 2012. After a brief period of working as a research assistant at the Department of Chemical Engineering under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Nilay Shah (link), I decided to look for PhD opportunities in yet a different country.

My search brought me to Zurich, Switzerland and to the Chair of Building Physics (link) of ETH Zurich (link) in 2013. In Zurich, over the next 3.5 years, I obtained my PhD degree under the supervision of [Prof. Dr. Jan Carmeliet][] and Dr.[Dr. Kristina Orehounig][]. My PhD research focused on the optimal design of distributed urban energy systems under uncertainty.
