### Research interests

My research revolves primarily around Building and Urban Energy Systems. More specifically, I focus on developing computational models and strategies for the transformation of buildings, urban districts and cities into efficient and sustainable energy systems. 

My main research interests include:

* The optimal model-based design of building and urban energy systems
* Uncertainty in the design of energy systems
* The integration of renewable energy with building and urban energy systems
* The interactions between building envelope retrofits and upgrades of building energy systems

In the paragraphs below you can find more detailed information about my [postdoctoral research](#postdoc), my [PhD thesis](#phd), and [earlier research activities](#earlyres).

### <a name="postdoc"></a>Postdoctoral research

My research activities as a Postdoctoral researcher at ETH Zurich are mainly under the Swiss Competence Center for Energy Research (SCCER) project [*"Future Energy Efficient Buildings & Districts (FEEB&D)"*](http://www.sccer-feebd.ch/). A topic I investigate within SCCER FEEB&D is the approaches to increase the local consumption of renewable electricity generated in urban areas, such as the integration of energy storage technologies and the inter-building energy exchange. An additional research area that I investigate relates to the strategic city energy system design. More specifically, I investigate multi-stage energy system design models that are able to reflect urban developments (*e.g.* new buildings, building renovations etc.) and will output a multi-phase plan for the transformation of the city’s energy system. The model development is currently performed in a deterministic manner; however, in the future, the aim is to introduce uncertainty in the model for more effective and robust energy system designs.

### <a name="phd"></a>PhD research

The focus of my recently completed PhD dissertation is the design of distributed energy systems (DES) under uncertainty. My thesis can be found [here](https://doi.org/10.3929/ethz-b-000182697). In order to investigate the impacts and the main drivers of uncertainty in DES design, I apply techniques of Uncertainty Analysis (UA) and Global Sensitivity Analysis (GSA) combined with state-of-the-art optimization models for DES design based on the energy hub concept. 

The insights gained via UA & GSA are then used to develop models for optimal decision-making under uncertainty based on techniques like Stochastic Programming and Robust Optimization. These techniques aim to identify optimal DES designs before uncertainty is revealed, while considering specific DES performance criteria against uncertainty (*e.g.* average or worst-case performance).

### <a name="earlyres"></a>Earlier research

Prior to my PhD studies, for my Diploma thesis in Aristotle University, I examined the operational impact of the application of Phase Change Materials (PCMs) on the outside of the refrigerator condenser coil. For my MSc project in Imperial College London, I developed a diagnostic tool based on Artificial Neural Networks (ANN) for energy performance evaluation and fault detection of a supermarket store and its systems in order to ensure optimal performance and minimise operating cost and environmental impacts. My MSc thesis was part of the [Imperial-Sainsbury's](http://www.imperial.ac.uk/sainsburys-decarbonisation-partnership/) and has been summarised in [this paper](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.enbuild.2013.03.020) published in *"Energy and Buildings"*.
